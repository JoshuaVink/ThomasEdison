Life Overview

From the life of Thomas Edison we see how God uses even wicked and unbelieving men to fulfill his will. Thomas Edison was one of the most influential inventors and businessmen in America. He was born February 11, 1847, and died October 18, 1931. He is best known for inventing the light bulb, but he also invented and improved many other devices that contribute to modern life. He achieved an amazing 1,093 patents for these inventions. They include the phonograph, motion picture camera, and improvements to the telegraph and telephone. He also used his skills as a businessman to advertise his inventions to the public. His life was full of corporations, business partnerships, and legal battles over patents.

Famous Quote

Thomas Edison: "I have not failed. I've just found 10,000 ways that won't work."
